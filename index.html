1. Introduction


1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this document is to specify the requirements of the Car Service and Management System. This system aims to facilitate the efficient management of car services including maintenance, repairs, scheduling, and inventory management.

1.2 Scope
The Car Service and Management System will provide functionalities for both customers and service providers. It will include features such as appointment scheduling, service history tracking, inventory management, billing, and reporting.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, and Abbreviations
SRS: Software Requirements Specification
CSS: Car Service and Management System

1.5 Overview
This document outlines the requirements of the Car Service and Management System in accordance with IEEE standards.

2. Overall Description


2.1 Product Perspective
The CSS will serve as a standalone system interacting with users through a user interface. It may integrate with external systems for functionalities like payment processing or vehicle diagnostic tools.

2.2 Product Features
Appointment scheduling
Service history tracking
Inventory management
Billing and invoicing
Reporting

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
Customers: Individuals seeking car services
Service Providers: Mechanics and technicians providing services

2.4 Operating Environment
The CSS will be web-based and accessible through modern web browsers. It will require a server environment for hosting.

The system shall be developed using [specific technologies/frameworks]. It should adhere to industry best practices and standards.

2.6 User Documentation
User manuals and online help resources will be provided to guide users in utilizing the system effectively.

2.7 Assumptions and Dependencies
The system assumes a stable internet connection for operation.

3.1 Feature 1: Appointment Scheduling
Customers can schedule appointments for car services.
Service providers can view and manage appointment schedules.
